# About Quantum Mechanics: Foundations: The Quantum World

GitHub repository: [quantum-mechanics-vol1](https://github.com/nikbearbrown/quantum-mechanics-vol1)

Author: Nik Bear Brown

## Book Summary

`quantum-mechanics-vol1` is organized as a chapter-based book project. The chapters below outline the book's main instructional arc and source files.

## Chapter Summary

1. **Chapter 1 — Why Classical Physics Failed: Blackbody, Photoelectric, and the Photon** (`chapters/01-why-classical-physics-failed.md`) - When iron is heated in a forge, it glows red, then orange, then yellow-white.
2. **Chapter 2 — Matter Waves: de Broglie, Davisson–Germer, and the Double Slit** (`chapters/02-matter-waves.md`) - Einstein had established that electromagnetic waves carry momentum $p = h/\lambda$.
3. **Chapter 3 — The Wave Function and Born's Rule** (`chapters/03-the-wave-function.md`) - In Chapter 0 we built a simulation that displayed a blue filled curve drifting across the screen, with an orange oscillating curve running through it.
4. **Chapter 4 — The Schrödinger Equation and Stationary States** (`chapters/04-the-schrodinger-equation.md`) - In January 1926, Erwin Schrödinger sought a wave equation for matter.
5. **Chapter 5 — The Infinite Square Well** (`chapters/05-the-infinite-square-well.md`) - In 1993, physicists at IBM's Almaden Research Center imaged the electron density inside a ring of 48 iron atoms they had arranged one by one on a copper surface using a scanning tunneling microscope.
6. **Chapter 6 — Finite Wells, Steps, and Barriers** (`chapters/06-finite-wells-steps-and-barriers.md`) - When we studied the infinite square well in Chapter 5, we assumed perfectly impenetrable walls.
7. **Chapter 7 — The Quantum Harmonic Oscillator** (`chapters/07-the-harmonic-oscillator.md`) - Imagine pulling a guitar string sideways by a small amount and letting go.
8. **Chapter 8 — The Free Particle and Wave Packets** (`chapters/08-the-free-particle-and-wave-packets.md`) - We begin with the simplest quantum mechanics problem there is: no potential anywhere, and a single particle moving freely through empty space.
9. **Chapter 9 — Operators and Uncertainty** (`chapters/09-operators-and-uncertainty.md`) - Consider an experiment we can carry out, at least in principle.
10. **Chapter 10 — Measurement, Superposition, and the Qubit** (`chapters/10-measurement-and-the-qubit.md`) - In this chapter we examine what happens when we make a measurement in quantum mechanics.
11. **Chapter 11 — Capstone: A 1D Quantum Sandbox** (`chapters/11-capstone-a-1d-quantum-sandbox.md`) - In this chapter we bring together the methods developed throughout the book by building a configurable one-dimensional Schrödinger solver.
