A Gaussian electron wave packet spreads not because quantum mechanics is uncertain about where it is, but because different momentum components travel at different speeds — the simulation measures the exact doubling time.

**The physics:** A Gaussian wave packet in free space spreads as σ(t) = σ₀√(1 + (ℏt/2mσ₀²)²). For σ₀ = 1 nm and electron mass, the doubling time — when the packet width reaches √2·σ₀ — is τ = 2mσ₀²/ℏ ≈ 17.3 fs. The simulation verifies that σ(τ) = 1.414 nm exactly and that the packet centroid moves at the classical group velocity v_g = ℏk₀/m throughout.
