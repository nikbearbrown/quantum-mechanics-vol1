# CAJAL Figure Report — Chapter 10 — Measurement, Superposition, and the Qubit

Recommended: 4 figures, High density.

Chapter 10 is rich with MC and PQ heuristics. The measurement postulate is a three-step causal process (MC): superposition goes in, outcome eigenvalue comes out, collapsed eigenstate remains — three causally ordered and interdependent steps that only make sense as a flow. The sequential Stern–Gerlach experiments (Z→Z, Z→X, Z→X→Z) are a structural comparison with a shared operational axis (MC + VG): three experimental configurations whose differing outputs are the argument, not decoration. The Bloch sphere is a textbook VG heuristic — a structural/spatial claim encoding two continuous degrees of freedom, the θ/2 mapping, and the correspondence between sphere geometry and physical observables, none of which are captured in prose or equation alone. The Born-rule probabilities for the worked example (three Paulis, numerical values computed exactly) are a PQ bar-chart heuristic: three quantitative comparison values on a shared scale from zero. A fifth candidate — the Robertson bound as a state-dependent floor in product-space — is absorbed into the Born-rule figure as a secondary annotation rather than warranting a standalone panel; the chapter's own simulation prompt treats it as a derived readout from the primary statistics display.
