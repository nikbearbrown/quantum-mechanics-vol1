# Quantum Mechanics Vol. 1: Foundations: The Quantum World

**Author:** Nik Bear Brown

---

## One-Sentence Pitch
The simulation-first first undergraduate volume that teaches the student to set up and solve any one-dimensional quantum problem — analytically and by simulation — and explain what the wave function means.

## The Argument
Quantum mechanics is hard because its objects have no classical analogue and the standard textbook gives no way to build intuition for them. This volume pairs every concept with an LLM-generated, student-modifiable D3 simulation, so the student sees each new object before manipulating its algebra. It is Volume 1 of the *Quantum Mechanics* series.

## The Gap
Griffiths/Townsend are rigorous but static; video and applets are visual but non-rigorous and unmodifiable. This volume gives the student a third object: a simulation they built, governed by physics they can derive, that they can change and watch respond.

## The Reader
Intro/Modern Physics → first undergraduate QM course (sophomore–junior). Enters with the prerequisites carried from the prior volume (or, for Volume 1, ODEs, complex numbers, and introductory mechanics/waves). Wants to *do* quantum mechanics, not just read about it. The optional **Quantum Mechanics Vol. 5 — Math for Quantum Physics** companion supplies any math refresher, indexed to the chapter the student is on.

## High-Level Outline
- Act One (1–3): the experiments force quantum mechanics into existence; the wave function and Born's rule arrive.
- Act Two (4–8): the Schrödinger equation and the canonical 1D systems — well, barrier, oscillator, wave packet.
- Act Three (9–11): operators, uncertainty, the first qubit, and a synthesis sandbox.
